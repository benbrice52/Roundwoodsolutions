Commercial Grass Cutting Contracts in Kent: A Guide for Facilities & Property Managers

← Back to blog
May 2026 By Roundwood Solutions Ltd 7 min read

Grass grows whether you're ready for it or not. For facilities managers, property managers, and business owners across Kent, keeping grassed areas looking professional throughout the season isn't just about aesthetics — it's about maintaining the value and presentation of your site, keeping paths and car parks safe, and meeting the expectations of tenants, clients, and visitors.

A well-structured commercial grass cutting contract takes that pressure off you entirely. But choosing the right contractor and getting the specification right from the start makes a significant difference to the quality and value you receive. This guide covers everything you need to know.

What Does a Commercial Grass Cutting Contract Include?

A commercial grass cutting contract is more than just someone turning up with a mower. A properly specified contract will typically cover:

  • Regular grass cutting — to an agreed height and finish, using the right equipment for your site. Large open areas require ride-on mowers for efficiency; tight borders and slopes need pedestrian or specialist equipment.
  • Strimming and edging — along paths, fences, kerbs, and planted border edges. This should always be included as standard, not an optional extra.
  • Green waste removal — all cuttings cleared and disposed of responsibly at the end of every visit. Sites that are left with grass clippings on hard surfaces or blown into borders are a sign the contractor isn't finishing the job properly.
  • Hard surface clearance — removing grass and weed growth from path edges, car park borders, and paving joints as part of each visit.
  • Seasonal variation — visit frequency should flex with the growing season. A good contract specifies fortnightly cuts during peak growth and reduced visits in winter, not a flat schedule that over-services in January and under-services in June.

One of the most common issues we see when taking on new contracts is that the previous specification was vague about what was actually included per visit. This leads to disagreements, additional charges, and a site that never looks quite right. Get the scope in writing from the outset.

How Often Should Commercial Grass Be Cut in Kent?

Frequency depends on the time of year, the grass type, and how formal the finish needs to be. As a practical guide for commercial sites in Kent:

  • March–April: First cuts of the season as growth resumes — typically fortnightly, with a spring tidy and edge reinstatement.
  • May–July: Peak growing season — fortnightly cuts as a minimum, weekly for formal lawns or high-visibility sites where presentation is critical.
  • August–September: Growth remains strong — fortnightly cuts, with additional attention to areas affected by summer dryness or heavy use.
  • October: Final cuts, leaf clearance begins, autumn border tidy alongside the last mow of the season.
  • November–February: Monthly or as-required visits — primarily for leaf clearance, winter inspections, and tidying after storms.

This amounts to roughly 18–22 visits per year for a typical commercial site in Kent. That figure should be written into your contract rather than left to the contractor's discretion — "as required" is not a specification.

Which Types of Commercial Property Need a Grass Cutting Contract?

We provide commercial grass cutting contracts across a wide range of property types in Kent and Medway. The requirements differ between them, but the need for reliable, professional maintenance is consistent.

Business Parks and Industrial Estates

Grass verges, planted borders, and amenity areas around business parks need to look well-maintained for tenants and visitors throughout the season. Contractors working on occupied estates also need to be considerate of access, parking, and working hours — factors that not all contractors manage well.

Schools and Education Settings

School grounds typically combine formal grass areas, playing fields, and planted zones. Cuts need to be timed around term-time use, with safe practices around children and staff. We work with schools across Medway and Kent and understand the specific requirements around access and scheduling.

Care Homes and Healthcare Sites

Grounds maintenance on care home and healthcare sites requires a quiet, considerate approach. Residents often use outdoor spaces throughout the day, so scheduling and noise levels need careful management. We carry out all work in a manner that minimises disruption.

Commercial Properties and Retail Sites

Office buildings, retail parks, and standalone commercial premises need grounds that reflect well on the business operating from them. A well-maintained external space makes a measurable difference to first impressions — both for customers and for staff.

Housing Associations and Managed Estates

Communal grass areas on managed residential estates need regular, reliable maintenance throughout the season. We work with housing associations and managing agents across Kent, providing consistent visits and clear reporting when issues arise.

Annual Contract vs Ad-Hoc Grass Cutting — Which Is Right for You?

Most commercial clients in Kent are better served by an annual contract than by booking cuts on a one-off basis. Here's why:

An annual contract fixes your price for the year, giving you budget certainty. It also means your site is prioritised in the schedule — ad-hoc bookings are fitted around existing contracts and can mean longer waits during the busiest parts of the season, which is exactly when you need cuts most.

Annual contracts also mean the same team visits your site throughout the year. They get to know the site — the awkward corner behind the bin store, the border that always catches the wind, the patch that drains slowly after rain. That familiarity translates into better quality and fewer missed details.

Ad-hoc grass cutting does have its place — for one-off clearances, overgrown sites being brought back to standard, or covering a gap between contractors. We offer both, but the vast majority of our commercial grass cutting work is done on annual contracts for exactly these reasons.

How Much Does Commercial Grass Cutting Cost in Kent?

Pricing for commercial grass cutting varies significantly depending on site size, access, cut frequency, and finish required. As a rough guide for sites across Kent and Medway:

  • Small commercial sites (up to 0.5 acres of grass): from £70–£130 per visit
  • Medium sites (0.5–2 acres): from £130–£280 per visit
  • Large sites and estates (2 acres+): priced on survey

Annual contracts are typically priced as a fixed monthly fee paid year-round, giving you a predictable cost that accounts for the full seasonal schedule rather than charging per visit. This works out more cost-effective than ad-hoc booking over the course of a year.

We provide itemised, fixed-price quotations following a free site survey — no vague estimates, no hidden extras, and no surprises mid-contract.

What to Look for in a Commercial Grass Cutting Contractor in Kent

Price is rarely the most important factor in a commercial grass cutting contract — reliability and quality of finish matter far more over a 12-month relationship. When evaluating contractors, look for:

  • A written specification — any contractor who can't provide a clear, written scope of what's included per visit isn't organised enough to deliver consistent results.
  • The same team on your site — familiarity with a site makes a real difference to the quality and efficiency of work. Ask whether you'll get a consistent crew or whoever happens to be available.
  • Adequate insurance — minimum £5M public liability as standard. This should be provided without being asked.
  • Relevant accreditation — BALI membership indicates a contractor operating to professional standards. PA1 and PA6A certification covers the safe application of herbicides and pesticides, which is relevant if weed control is part of your contract.
  • Local knowledge — a contractor based in Kent who understands local growing conditions, seasonal patterns, and the specific challenges of the area will serve you better than a national firm managing your site remotely.
  • Clear communication — a contractor who flags issues proactively (a dying shrub, an emerging drainage problem, damage to a border caused by a delivery vehicle) is worth far more than one who simply completes the visit and leaves.
