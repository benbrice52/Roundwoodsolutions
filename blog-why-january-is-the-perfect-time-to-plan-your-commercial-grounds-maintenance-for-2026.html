Why January Is the Perfect Time to Plan Your Commercial Grounds Maintenance for 2026

← Back to blog
Roundwood Solutions Ltd January 2026 Grounds Maintenance

January might seem like an odd time to think about grounds maintenance — the grass is barely growing and your site looks dormant. But for commercial property managers and facilities teams in Kent, January is actually the best time to get your grounds maintenance arrangements in place for the year ahead.

Contractor Availability

Professional grounds maintenance contractors in Kent are significantly busier from March onwards as the growing season begins. Contractors who take on new clients in January can plan schedules properly, allocate the right team to your site, and ensure you get the visit frequency you need from the start of the season. Leaving it until March or April often means taking whatever slots are left — or being turned away altogether by contractors who are already at capacity.

Site Surveys and Proper Planning

Getting a site survey done in January means there is time to assess your grounds properly, discuss your requirements, agree a specification, and have a written contract in place before the growing season starts. Rushing this process in spring often leads to mismatched expectations and poor results in the first few months of service.

Budget Planning

Many commercial organisations need to plan their facilities budgets at the start of the year. Having a confirmed grounds maintenance contract in place in January — with fixed, agreed pricing — makes this much easier. Annual contracts with monthly payments spread the cost evenly and eliminate surprise invoices throughout the year.

Winter Works

January and February are also a good time to get any winter maintenance works done — site clearance, hard surface repairs, fencing work, or any landscaping projects that are easier to complete before the growing season begins. Getting these done now means your site is in the best possible condition when spring arrives.
